# Compliance

**Last Updated:** January 2026

## 1. Regulatory Framework Overview

Trusted AI Advisory Inc. is committed to operating in full compliance with Canadian federal and provincial regulations governing AI deployment, data protection, and public sector operations.

Our advisory services align with:

- Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS) Directive on Automated Decision-Making
- Government of Canada AI principles and guidelines
- Provincial regulatory frameworks as applicable

## 2. AI Ethics & Governance Principles

We adhere to the following core principles in all our AI advisory work:

- **Transparency:** AI systems should be explainable and auditable
- **Accountability:** Clear lines of responsibility for AI-driven decisions
- **Fairness:** Prevention of bias and discrimination in AI outputs
- **Human Oversight:** Maintaining human control over critical decisions
- **Privacy by Design:** Embedding privacy protections from the outset

## 3. Data Protection

We comply with applicable data protection legislation, including:

- **PIPEDA** (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
- **Provincial privacy laws** (e.g., Quebec's Law 25, Alberta's PIPA, BC's PIPA)
- **Public sector privacy acts** as applicable to our government clients

## 4. Security Standards & Certifications

Our approach to security includes:

- Secure development practices for all prototypes
- Data encryption in transit and at rest
- Access controls and authentication measures
- Regular security assessments

## 5. Audit & Accountability Measures

We maintain:

- Documentation of all AI system recommendations
- Traceability of decision-making processes
- Regular internal compliance reviews
- Cooperation with client audit requirements

## 6. Third-Party Vendor Compliance

When engaging third-party tools or services, we ensure:

- Vendor due diligence assessments
- Contractual privacy and security requirements
- Compliance with client-specific vendor policies
- Data residency requirements are met

## 7. Incident Reporting Procedures

In the event of a security or privacy incident:

- Immediate assessment and containment
- Notification to affected parties as required by law
- Cooperation with regulatory authorities
- Post-incident review and remediation
